18Nov

Day 1 — Arrival in Taipei 🛫

Cebu → Taipei Taoyuan International Airport

Travel Day ▾
Early AM
Depart Mactan–Cebu International Airport (CEB)
Check in at least 2.5 hours before departure. Most CEB→TPE flights operate late morning to late afternoon. Recommended airlines: EVA Air, STARLUX, or China Airlines. Flight duration: ~2h 50m. Arrive at Taoyuan International Airport (TPE), Terminal 2.
Flight: ₱8,000–₱14,000 one-way (book early)
Afternoon
Arrive Taoyuan Airport → Take Airport MRT to Taipei
Clear immigration and customs. Purchase an EasyCard (悠遊卡) at the MRT station — this reloadable card works on Taipei MRT, buses, and convenience stores. The Airport MRT Express Line gets you to Taipei Main Station in just 35 minutes. Cost: NT$150 (~₱294).
Airport MRT Express: NT$150 (~₱294)
3–5 PM
Check In — Hotel near Ximending/Ximen MRT
Recommended: Just Sleep Taipei Ximending (stylish mid-range, directly above Ximen MRT Station, NT$2,800–₱5,488/night) or Orange Hotel Hanzhong (budget-friendly, NT$1,800–₱3,528/night). Both are perfectly placed for sightseeing.
Hotel 2 nights Taipei: ~₱7,000–₱11,000 total
5–7 PM
Freshen Up & Explore Ximending (西門町)
Walk out of your hotel directly into Taipei's most vibrant pedestrian district — often called the "Harajuku of Taipei." Browse quirky shops, pick up K-pop and BTS merchandise, enjoy street performers, and soak in the neon-lit evening energy.
7–9 PM
Dinner at Ximending Street Food
Try Ah-Chung Flour-Rice Noodle (a 40-year Taipei institution), scallion pancakes, oyster vermicelli, or beef noodle soup. End with bubble tea from Spring Water Shark or Tiger Sugar — Taiwan is the birthplace of bubble tea! Dinner for one: NT$150–300.
Dinner: NT$200–400 (~₱392–₱784)
19Nov

Day 2 — Taipei Highlights 🏯

Taipei 101 · CKS Memorial · Longshan Temple · Night Market

Explore ▾
7:30 AM
Taiwanese Breakfast
Start like a local at a traditional breakfast shop (早餐店). Order 蛋餅 (egg crepe), 燒餅油條 (sesame flatbread with fried dough), and warm soy milk (豆漿). Abundant, delicious, and dirt cheap. Find one near your hotel — they're everywhere.
Breakfast: NT$80–150 (~₱157–₱294)
9 AM
Taipei 101 Observation Deck (台北101)
Ride the world's fastest elevator (60 floors in 37 seconds!) to the 89th-floor observation deck for breathtaking panoramic views over the entire Taipei basin. The iconic bamboo-shaped tower was once the world's tallest building. The massive 660-tonne steel damper ball inside is a sight in itself. Book tickets online to skip the queue.
Entry: NT$600 (~₱1,176)
11 AM
Elephant Mountain Hike (象山)
Just a 15-min MRT ride from 101, this 183-meter urban peak offers one of Taipei's most iconic skyline shots — Taipei 101 framed against the city. The hike up is steep (300+ steps) but takes only 30–40 minutes. Arrive before noon for the best light and fewer crowds. Wear comfortable shoes!
Free
12:30 PM
Lunch near Xinyi District
Head to the Xinyi shopping district near Taipei 101 for lunch. Try Din Tai Fung (鼎泰豐) if you want the world-famous xiao long bao (soup dumplings) — expect a queue. Or grab a cheaper local set lunch at any nearby restaurant.
Lunch: NT$250–500 (~₱490–₱980)
2 PM
Chiang Kai-Shek Memorial Hall & Liberty Square (中正紀念堂)
One of Taipei's grandest landmarks — a gleaming white marble building with a distinctive blue octagonal roof, symbolizing good fortune. Set within the spacious Liberty Square, flanked by the National Theater and Concert Hall. Don't miss the hourly changing of the guard ceremony (daily 9am–5pm). Free entry.
Free
4 PM
Longshan Temple (龍山寺)
Taipei's most visited temple, built in 1738. This ornate masterpiece of classical Taiwanese architecture is alive with incense smoke and the sound of prayers. Devotees come to worship Guanyin and dozens of other deities. The intricate stone carvings and golden altars are extraordinary. A living, breathing spiritual heart of old Taipei.
Free (donations welcome)
5 PM
Dihua Street (迪化街) — Old Town Charm
Stroll along Taipei's most historic commercial street, lined with beautiful Baroque-style facades from the Japanese colonial era. Pick up dried fruits, traditional tea, and unique souvenirs. Excellent for gifts to bring home.
7 PM
Raohe Night Market (饒河夜市)
One of Taipei's most atmospheric night markets, stretching 600 meters along the street. The famous Black Pepper Bun stall at the entrance (天福堂胡椒餅) is a must — the buns are baked in a charcoal-fired barrel and sell out fast. Also try stinky tofu, grilled corn, fried sweet potato balls, and herbal stewed pork ribs soup (藥燉排骨).
Street food budget: NT$300–500 (~₱588–₱980)
20Nov

Day 3 — Taipei → Kaohsiung 🚄

HSR Bullet Train · Lotus Pond · Pier-2 Art Center · Concert Eve

Travel + Explore ▾
7 AM
Check Out & Head to Taipei Main Station
Store any luggage you don't need (many hotels hold bags). Take the MRT or a short walk to Taipei Main Station where the THSR (High Speed Rail) departs. Grab a quick breakfast at the station's food court or a nearby convenience store (7-Eleven has excellent ready-made meals in Taiwan).
8–10 AM
THSR Bullet Train — Taipei → Zuoying (Kaohsiung)
Board Taiwan's sleek High Speed Rail, which zips from Taipei to Kaohsiung in just 1 hour 35 minutes at up to 300km/h. Trains depart frequently. Purchase tickets at the counter (bring your passport) or book in advance via Klook for a discounted fare of NT$1,265. Sit on the right side (west) for ocean glimpses along the coast.
THSR Ticket: NT$1,265–1,490 (~₱2,479–₱2,920)
10 AM
Arrive Zuoying Station → Check In to Hotel
Recommended hotels near the stadium & Zuoying HSR Station: Kaohsiung Marriott Hotel (luxury, connected to Zuoying Station, ~₱8,500/night) or Long Siang Hotel (3-star, 3-min walk to Kaohsiung Arena MRT, ~₱3,500/night). Drop luggage and freshen up.
Hotel 2 nights Kaohsiung: ~₱7,000–₱17,000 total
11 AM
Lotus Pond (蓮池潭) & Dragon-Tiger Pagodas
Kaohsiung's most iconic landmark. A beautiful lotus-filled lake in Zuoying district featuring the legendary Dragon and Tiger Pagodas (龍虎塔) — you enter through the dragon's mouth and exit through the tiger's tail to ward off bad luck. Also visit the Spring and Autumn Pavilions connected by a zigzag bridge over the water. A photographer's dream at any time of day.
Free (5-min taxi or MRT from Zuoying Station)
1 PM
Lunch — Local Kaohsiung Specialties
Try Kaohsiung's signature dishes: rice noodles (米糕), braised pork rice (爌肉飯), or fresh seafood near the harbor. Wander near the harbor area for casual eateries with local flavor.
Lunch: NT$150–300 (~₱294–₱588)
3 PM
Pier-2 Art Center (駁二藝術特區)
A transformed 1970s warehouse district turned into one of Taiwan's most beloved creative spaces. Explore quirky art installations, colorful murals, independent boutiques, and open-air sculpture gardens. The Banana Pier area nearby is perfect for a walk. Great for photos! An essential piece of Kaohsiung's modern cultural identity.
Free entry to grounds (some indoor exhibitions NT$50–150)
6 PM
Sunset at Sizihwan (西子灣)
Kaohsiung's most romantic sunset spot. Watch the sky turn gold and crimson over the harbor from this iconic bay and former British Consulate site. Locals love to stroll here in the evening. Keep an eye on your watch — you'll want to leave time for rest!
8 PM
Dinner at Liuhe Night Market (六合夜市)
Kaohsiung's most famous night market, stretching 390m along Liuhe 2nd Road. Famous for massive grilled fresh seafood, papaya milk (木瓜牛奶 — a Kaohsiung classic!), and roasted milk tea. Try the century egg with spicy sauce or steamed shrimp dumplings. A feast before the big day!
Dinner: NT$300–500 (~₱588–₱980)
10 PM
Early Rest — Big Day Tomorrow! 🌙
Get to bed early. The BTS concert is tomorrow — you'll want to be energized, hydrated, and ready to sing your heart out. Prepare your outfit, charge all devices, print or download your ticket, and pack your essentials bag tonight.
21Nov

Day 4 — BTS ARIRANG Concert Night 🎤

Kaohsiung National Stadium · World Games MRT Station

Concert Day ▾
9 AM
Slow Morning — Fuel Up Right
Sleep in if you need it! Have a hearty breakfast — you'll be standing and dancing for hours tonight. Head to a nearby breakfast shop or your hotel's restaurant. Hydrate well throughout the day. This is your recovery and preparation morning.
Breakfast: NT$150–250 (~₱294–₱490)
11 AM
Concert Merchandise Line
Official BTS merchandise lines open early — sometimes 4–6 hours before showtime. If you want to buy lightsticks, apparel, or keychains, head to the stadium area early in the morning. Queues for popular items can exceed 2–3 hours. Bring a portable charger, snacks, and water for the wait.
Merchandise budget: ₱1,000–₱5,000 (optional)
1 PM
Lunch + Rest at Hotel
Have a good lunch at a restaurant near your hotel. Avoid heavy food that might upset your stomach during the concert. Then rest for 1–2 hours — concerts typically run 2.5–3.5 hours and you'll be on your feet the entire time!
Lunch: NT$200–350 (~₱392–₱686)
4 PM
Head to Kaohsiung National Stadium
Take the Kaohsiung MRT Red Line to World Games Station (R17) — the station is right at the stadium. Alternatively, shuttle buses run from Zuoying HSR Station for large events. Arrive at least 2 hours early to find your seat, use restrooms, and soak in the pre-concert atmosphere. Remember to bring your valid photo ID — the concert uses a real-name ticketing system!
MRT: NT$30–50 (~₱59–₱98)
7 PM
🎤 BTS WORLD TOUR 'ARIRANG' — SHOWTIME
The moment you've been waiting for. Kaohsiung National Stadium (capacity ~55,000) fills with 55,000 ARMYs as BTS takes the stage for the first time since all members completed military service. Expect electrifying performances of both new ARIRANG album tracks and beloved classics like Fire, Fake Love, Dynamite, and Butter. Sing every word, wave your ARMY Bomb, and make memories that last a lifetime. The concert typically runs 2.5–3.5 hours.
11 PM+
Post-Concert Return to Hotel
The MRT will be crowded post-concert but Kaohsiung's system handles it well — locals report relatively smooth boarding even after big events. Expect 20–30 minutes on the Red Line back toward the city center. Grab late-night street food or ramen near your hotel to celebrate.
22Nov

Day 5 — Kaohsiung Sights + Return to Taipei 🚄

Formosa Blvd · Love River · Return to Taipei for last night

Chill + Travel ▾
9 AM
Sleep In — You've Earned It! ☀️
Let the post-concert glow settle in. Sleep as late as you need. Have a leisurely hotel breakfast or walk to a local café. This is a lighter day. Relive last night's memories and share your BTS concert photos with friends back home.
11 AM
Formosa Boulevard MRT Station — Dome of Light (美麗島站)
Considered one of the most beautiful MRT stations in the world, Formosa Boulevard station features the "Dome of Light" — a stunning 2,145 square-meter stained glass installation by Italian-American artist Narcissus Quagliata depicting the four elements. It's absolutely free to visit and photograph. Right in the heart of the city on the MRT.
Free
12:30 PM
Lunch + Stroll along Love River (愛河)
Kaohsiung's signature riverside promenade, lined with restaurants, coffee shops, and romantic scenery. Enjoy a riverside lunch at one of the many cafés, then take a leisurely walk. The Love River cruise (NT$80) offers a different perspective of the city if you're feeling adventurous.
Lunch: NT$200–400 (~₱392–₱784) + optional cruise NT$80
2 PM
Last-Minute Souvenir Shopping
Pick up Kaohsiung specialties: pineapple cakes (鳳梨酥), sun cakes (太陽餅), dried mango slices, papaya milk mix, and local tea. The area around Sanduo Shopping District or Dream Mall has good options. Don't overstuff your bag — save room for Taipei shopping!
4 PM
Check Out & THSR Back to Taipei
Head back to Zuoying HSR Station and take the afternoon bullet train back to Taipei (~1h 35m). Check into your final night's hotel near Taipei Main Station or Ximending. The Westgate Hotel, Just Sleep Ximending, or any previous hotel works perfectly.
THSR Ticket: NT$1,265–1,490 (~₱2,479–₱2,920)
7 PM
Farewell Taipei Dinner
Your last evening in Taiwan! Splurge a little on a memorable dinner. Options: the famous beef noodle soup at Lin Dong Fang (林東芳牛肉麵), a classic Taiwanese hot pot, or revisit your favorite spot from Day 2. Finish with one last bubble tea for the road.
Dinner: NT$300–600 (~₱588–₱1,176)
9 PM
Shilin Night Market Option (士林夜市)
If you still have energy, Shilin is Taipei's most famous night market — massive, lively, and famous for oyster vermicelli, fried chicken cutlets, and Taiwanese shaved ice desserts. Take the MRT Red Line to Jiantan Station. Open until midnight.
NT$200–400 (~₱392–₱784)
23Nov

Day 6 — Departure Day ✈️

Taipei → Taoyuan Airport → Cebu

Departure ▾
7 AM
Morning Routine & Hotel Checkout
Have a final Taiwanese breakfast — make it count! Check out of your hotel. If your flight is in the afternoon, most hotels will hold your luggage for free while you explore a little more.
9 AM
Optional: Last-Minute Activities
If time allows: visit the National Palace Museum (故宮博物院) which houses one of the world's greatest collections of Chinese imperial art and jade artifacts, or stroll Da'an Forest Park for a peaceful morning under the trees. Both are easy MRT rides from the city center.
National Palace Museum: NT$350 (~₱686)
2–3 PM
Head to Taoyuan Airport (TPE)
Allow at least 3 hours before your departure. Take the Airport MRT Express from Taipei Main Station to Terminal 2 (35 minutes, NT$150). Check in, clear immigration, and browse the excellent duty-free shops — Taiwan airports are famous for great last-minute pineapple cake gift sets!
Airport MRT: NT$150 (~₱294)
Evening
Depart Taipei → Arrive Cebu 🏠
Board your flight back to Cebu (CEB). The ~2h 50m flight gives you time to scroll through all your concert photos and videos. Upon landing at Mactan–Cebu International, you'll arrive home full of incredible memories of BTS, bubble tea, and Taiwan's warm hospitality. 감사합니다, ARMY! 보라해! 💜

WHAT NOT TO BRING

Essential Travel Tips

Everything you need to know to travel Taiwan like a pro.

🆓

Visa-Free Entry

Philippine passport holders enjoy visa-free entry to Taiwan for up to 90 days. Just make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates.

💳

EasyCard (悠遊卡)

Buy an EasyCard at any MRT station for NT$100 deposit. Load credit and use it for MRT, buses, YouBike rentals, and even convenience stores. Essential for getting around both Taipei and Kaohsiung.

📱

SIM / eSIM

Buy a 5-day or 7-day tourist SIM at Taoyuan Airport (Chunghwa Telecom, FarEasTone) from NT$300–500, or pre-load an eSIM via Airalo app before departure. Unlimited data available.

💱

Currency Exchange

Exchange PHP to TWD at Taoyuan Airport (decent rates) or at banks in Taipei. Current rate: ~₱1.96 per NT$1. Bring some cash — many local restaurants and night market stalls are cash-only.

🌡️

November Weather

November is one of Taiwan's most pleasant months — warm (20–26°C), low humidity, mostly sunny. Pack light layers for cool evenings. Rain is possible but not common. Perfect concert weather!

🍍

Pineapple Cakes (鳳梨酥)

Taiwan's most beloved souvenir. Buy from Sunny Hills (微熱山丘) or Chia Te (佳德) bakery in Taipei — both are considered the gold standard. They make perfect pasalubong gifts back home!

🚲

YouBike (台灣好行)

Taipei and Kaohsiung both have excellent public bike-sharing systems. Use your EasyCard to rent a YouBike from stations across the city — first 30 minutes is free! Great for short distances.

🏪

7-Eleven / FamilyMart

Taiwan has the world's highest convenience store density. Open 24/7, they sell hot meals, onigiri, ATM services, ticket pickup (including concert tickets via ibon kiosk!), and more. Use them freely.

🗣️

Language

Mandarin Chinese is official; Taiwanese Hokkien widely spoken. Most MRT signs and tourist areas are in English. Google Translate's camera mode is very useful for menus. Locals are extremely helpful even with a language barrier.

🚄 THSR Booking Tip: Pre-book your Taiwan High Speed Rail tickets on Klook or KKday for a discounted fare of NT$1,265 (vs. the counter price of NT$1,490). You'll need to exchange for physical tickets at the station counter using your passport. Book both legs (Taipei→Kaohsiung and Kaohsiung→Taipei) before your trip.
⚡ Emergency Info: Emergency number in Taiwan: 110 (Police) / 119 (Fire & Ambulance). Taiwan Tourism Bureau Hotline (English): 0800-011-765. Philippine AIT/Consulate Taipei: +886-2-2509-6000.
